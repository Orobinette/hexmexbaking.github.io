Overview



Prep Time: 45 minutes

Proof time: 2 hours

Cook time: 15 minutes

Total time: 3 hours


Yield: 16 rolls


Ingredients



Tangzhong

  • 70g milk*
  • 70g water
  • 25g flour

Dough

  • 100g milk
  • 100g water
  • 25g sugar
  • 1 package instant yeast

  • 425g bread flour
  • 10g salt
  • 2 tbsp butter, softened

Topping

  • 3 tbsp melted butter*
  • Coarse salt

Directions



  1. Make the tangzhong
    1. Add water, milk, and flour to a pot
    2. Whisk until no clumps remain
    3. Cook on medium heat, constantly stirring* until the mixture thickens into a porridge-like texture and the spoon creates streaks on the bottom of the pit
  2. Combine tangzhong, milk, water, yeast, and sugar in a large bowl
  3. Add flour and salt
  4. Add butter
  5. Knead 8-10 minutes
  6. Proof 1 hr
  7. Shape into 20 rolls and place in a 9x13 baking pan
  8. Preheat oven to 400 F and proof for another 30 m to 1 hr
  9. Brush with melted butter and sprinkle with sea salt
  10. Cook for 13-15 minutes (broiler for last 2 minutes for a more golden top)*

Notes



  • Non-dairy milks also work for tangzhong
  • I usually switch to a spoon while cooking tangzhong because the paste gets stuck in my whisk
  • I wash with melted butter to give a more buttery flavour and softer crust, which was my goal for this recipe. Other washes, like milk or egg, work as well, they'll just alter the color, flavor, and texture a little.
  • Butter wash naturally gets less brown, which is why I broil. If using egg wash, no need to broil
